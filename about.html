Founder and Lawyer

Jason Poirier Lavoie

Called to the Quebec Bar in 2023, Jason Poirier Lavoie articled and practiced with the law firm Allali Brault. He has represented clients before all levels of Quebec's judiciary, including the Québec Court of Appeal. He has pleaded in a variety of matters, including disputes related to conveyance of titles, real-estate broker and attorney liability, construction fraud, and shareholder conflicts. He now is a contract and risk manager at CAE, where his practice centers on commercial contract law and risk management.

Committed to access to justice, he volunteers with the Legislative Research Committee of the Young Bar of Montreal, provides pro bono counsel at legal clinics, and facilitates educational workshops for Éducaloi. He also supports the local cultural sector as President of the Board of Director for Playwright's Workshop Montréal.

Before entering the legal profession, Jason Poirier Lavoie built a career spanning over a decade in the aviation and defense industries. He served as a combat engineer in the Canadian Armed Forces, then transitioned to the private sector where he gained operational experience at Air Canada and technical expertise as a technologist in the CSeries aircraft program at Bombardier. His pre-law career culminated in a project management role at the consulting firm Aviation Strategies International, providing him with a comprehensive understanding of the industry's operational and strategic challenges.

Jason Poirier Lavoie is currently a candidate for a Master of Laws in Business Law at the University of Montréal, with his studies concentrated in banking, securities, and finance. He earned his Bachelor of Laws from the same institution ('21). Prior to his legal studies, he obtained a Bachelor of Arts, with distinction, Honours in Political Science from Concordia University ('17) and holds a cégep diploma in aircraft maintenance from the École Nationale d'Aérotechnique ('12).
